Aya is a mature, fairly no-nonsense kind of girl. Generally level-headed, she nonetheless can be impatient and distant, failing to understand the emotional complexity of a situation. She’s a quick, decisive thinker and isn’t afraid to speak her mind about an issue, unconcerned about offending people. If you need an honest, to-the-point perspective, she a good person to ask, though be prepared for her somewhat abrasive remarks. Despite this, she’s a natural leader, and her skills make up for her rough personality, and she’s generally well-liked and respected. When her followers manage to annoy her, she has been known to simply dismiss them and take on the problem herself.

Appearance

She wears her dark red hair in a drill style, with a small curl framing each side of her face and a single wide tress to the middle of her back.

Gown

Her Maiden gown is a black V-neck dress with short, frilled sleeves that ends in a ruffle skirt just above her knees. A thin amber sash is tied in a bow under her chest.

High School (Four Years Ago)

When she was in high school, Aya wore her hair slightly shorter. Her high school uniform was a simple yellow/cream blazer with a black skirt.

Maiden Abilities

Aya is a broadly well-rounded character, though her true strength is her magic at the slight cost of her physical attributes. This explains her preferred use of a bow—a ranged weapon—rather than something like a sword. She displays great mastery of spells, specializing in area-of-effect spells, allowing for crowd-control that compensates for her avoidance of close-quarters combat. Her archery skills are naturally quite high, and she can also loosely control the path of her fired arrows, though doing so requires a large amount of concentration.

Though a wind Maiden by nature, she also has some basic competence in electric magic. As such, she is one of very few Maidens throughout history who have shown proficiency in multiple magical elements.

Trivia

  • Character appearance references:
    • Her general apperance is inspired by this recolored image of Saki Tenjouin from the anime Motto to Love-Ru. (Original Image)
    • Her Gown is based on this dress, sold here.
  • Origin of spell names: Her spell names are largely derived from the Ace Combat video game series, most notably from the player squadron's wingmen in Ace Combat 5: The Unsung War.
    • Urimi — from Hans Grimm, callsign "Archer", who flies as Wardog/Razgriz 4. In particular, the name comes from Grimm's Japanese name, グリム( Gurimu ) . Since Aya's choice of weapon is a bow, activating her transformation makes her an archer, creating a direct connection to Grimm's callsign.
    • Induís — from ウィンドウィスパー( Wind Whisper ) , the Japanese name [EN: "Wind's Whisper"] of the finishing burst of Jessica Philomele in Mana Khemia: Alchemists of Al-Revis. The spells themselves (Induís and Wind's Whisper) are not particularly similar in effect: Jess fuses with the Mana of Wind to deal large damage.
    • Ké — from Kei Nagase, callsign "Edge", who flies as Wardog/Razgriz 2. The spell's effect of creating sharp guests of wind broadly resembles the slashing of a sword, connecting to Nagase's callsign. This is also a reference to the anime Girly Air Force, whose main character (also a fighter pilot in a dual lead/supporting role) is named Narutani Kei.
    • Alvia — from Alvin H. Davenport, callsign "Chopper", who flies as Wardog 3. The spell's effect of pushing things away from her is related to Davenport's callsign, with the blades of a helicopter resembling the blades of a fan.
    • Sàndrahed — from AWACS Thunderhead, who serves as the AWACS for Wardog Squadron. In particular, the spell's name comes from the callsign in the Japanese version of the game: AWACS サンダーヘッド( Sandāheddo (Thunderhead) ) . This reference ties to both Aya's normal wind element (as an Ace Combat reference consistent with her wind-element spells) and her dabbling into electric-element spells (as the word "thunder" ∼ electric).
      This spell is also broadly similar to the Pokémon move Electroweb.
  • The animal component of her callsign, Fox, also refers to the word's use as a brevity/code word by NATO pilots. In this context, it signifies that a pilot has fired a (usually air-to-air) missile. As an archer, her use of a bow resembles the firing of a missile.
    • Her aim is naturally quite good, but because her shots can also be loosely controlled by her magic, the most accurate firing call is "Fox Two", used for infrared-guided missiles.
